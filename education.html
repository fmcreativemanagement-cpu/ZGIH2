Key Facts

What you should know about
plastics & waste

🧴 500 years

How Long Plastic Takes to Decompose

Most plastic items take 400–500 years to break down in a landfill. They don't disappear — they fragment into microplastics that enter the food chain.

🌊 8 million tons

Plastic Entering the Ocean Annually

An estimated 8 million metric tons of plastic enters the world's oceans every year — much of it from coastal communities in Southeast Asia.

🇵🇭 Top 3

Philippines Among World's Top Plastic Polluters

The Philippines is consistently ranked among the top contributors to ocean plastic globally, largely due to single-use sachet packaging.

♻️ 9% only

Of All Plastic Ever Produced Has Been Recycled

Of the 9.1 billion tons of plastic produced since 1950, only about 9% has been recycled. The rest is in landfills, oceans, or the environment.

🐠 1 in 3

Marine Species Affected by Plastic Debris

Marine turtles, seabirds, fish, and marine mammals are affected by plastic pollution — ingesting it or becoming entangled in discarded fishing gear.

💡 68%

Reduction in Waste When Communities Segregate

Studies in Philippine barangays show that proper waste segregation at the source can divert up to 68% of household waste from landfills.
