PHASE 1

Weeks 1–2

Partnership Formation

Every successful program starts with a clear understanding of what the corporate partner needs and what ZGIH can deliver. This phase turns interest into a formal, committed partnership agreement.

📞

Step 1.1 Initial Inquiry & Discovery Call

The company reaches out via ZGIH's website, Facebook, or email. A ZGIH program officer schedules a 30-minute discovery call within 2 business days to understand the company's sustainability goals, EPR obligations (if any), preferred program type, budget range, and timeline.

ZGIH asks about your company's plastic footprint, product categories, and sustainability commitments We clarify whether the program is voluntary CSR or mandatory EPR compliance under RA 11898 We discuss preferred geography (city-wide, specific barangays, coastal, schools) and any branding requirements
💡 EPR Check: If your company produces, imports, or sells plastic-packaged goods in the Philippines, you may have legal recovery targets under RA 11898. ZGIH will help you assess your compliance status in this call — free of charge.
📄

Step 1.2 Needs Assessment & Site Visit

For programs above the Seedling tier, ZGIH conducts a brief needs assessment — reviewing the company's existing sustainability data, current waste volumes (if EPR), and any preferred communities or beneficiaries. For larger programs, a site visit to ZGIH's operations in Zamboanga City is offered.

Review of company's plastic use data, product categories, and annual production volume (for EPR calculation) Assessment of which ZGIH program components best match the company's goals Identification of target barangays, schools, or coastal areas for the program Optional site visit to ZGIH's community operations and upcycling workshop
🤝

Step 1.3 Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) Signing

Once both parties are aligned, ZGIH prepares a formal Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) outlining program targets, timelines, financial terms, branding rights, reporting obligations, and the roles of each party. Signing makes the partnership official.

MOA drafted by ZGIH legal representative within 5 business days Terms include: waste diversion targets (in kg/tons), program duration, payment schedule, and deliverables For EPR programs, the MOA is structured to be aligned with DENR reporting requirements Co-signed by ZGIH Executive Director and company's authorized representative
✅ Transparency commitment: ZGIH publishes all active partnerships (with consent) on our website's Partners Directory, holding ourselves publicly accountable to the stated targets.
📋

Phase 1 Deliverables

What the partner receives at the end of this phase:

  • Signed Memorandum of Agreement (MOA)
  • Program overview document with targets, timeline, and milestones
  • Assigned ZGIH Program Officer (your dedicated point of contact)
  • Welcome kit with ZGIH brand assets and co-branding guidelines
PHASE 2

Weeks 2–4

Program Design & Planning

With the MOA in place, ZGIH's team designs the full operational plan — mapping out exactly who does what, where, when, and how impact will be measured. Nothing is left to chance.

🗺️

Step 2.1 Target Area & Community Mapping

ZGIH maps out the specific barangays, coastal areas, or schools where the program will operate. Community mapping includes existing waste infrastructure (MRFs, collection points), key community leaders, and volunteer networks.

GIS-based mapping of target barangays and collection zones in Zamboanga City Coordination with Barangay Captains and Barangay Environment Officers Identification of existing MRF (Material Recovery Facilities) and junk shop partners Baseline waste audit in target areas to establish pre-program benchmarks
📊

Step 2.2 Setting Targets & KPIs

ZGIH's Research Officer works with the corporate partner to set clear, measurable Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) that align with both the program goals and the partner's ESG/CSR reporting framework.

ENVIRONMENTAL KPIs

  • Total waste collected (kg)
  • Waste diverted from landfill (kg)
  • Plastic type breakdown (%)
  • Number of clean-up events
  • Mangroves planted (if applicable)

SOCIAL KPIs

  • Beneficiaries reached
  • Artisans trained & employed
  • Students educated
  • Volunteer hours logged
  • Barangays engaged

EPR KPIs (if applicable)

  • Recovery tonnage vs. legal target
  • Plastic types recovered by resin code
  • DENR-reportable waste data
  • Certified diversion records
  • Compliance percentage
📅

Step 2.3 Activity Calendar & Operational Plan

ZGIH prepares a month-by-month activity calendar covering all program events, data collection schedules, reporting dates, and community engagement activities. This becomes the operational backbone of the program.

Month 1: Mobilization & Baseline
Month 2–3: First Collection Drive
Month 4–6: Ongoing Operations
Month 6: Mid-Program Review
Month 12: Final Report
🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Step 2.4 Team Assignment & Responsibility Matrix

ZGIH assigns a dedicated program team and defines exactly who is responsible for every program component — both on the ZGIH side and the corporate partner side.

📋

Phase 2 Deliverables

  • Full Program Operational Plan (PDF)
  • 12-month Activity Calendar with milestones
  • KPI Framework & measurement methodology
  • Target community map (geo-referenced)
  • Responsibility matrix & team contacts
PHASE 3

Weeks 3–5 (ongoing)

Community Mobilization

The program only works if the community is genuinely engaged. ZGIH has built trusted relationships with barangay leaders, fisherfolk, school principals, and local volunteers across Zamboanga City — this phase activates those networks.

🏘️

Step 3.1 Barangay Coordination & Community Consent

ZGIH's Community Liaison meets with Barangay Captains, Barangay Environment Officers (BEOs), and homeowners' associations in target areas. This is not a top-down operation — community leaders co-design how the program runs in their barangay.

Formal community consultation meeting held in each target barangay Barangay resolutions or endorsement letters secured for official documentation Community needs and concerns incorporated into the program design Local volunteer leaders identified and oriented on the program goals
🤝 Why this matters: Programs that skip community consent often face resistance or low participation. ZGIH's deep roots in Zamboanga communities are what make our collection drives consistently exceed targets.
📢

Step 3.2 Awareness Campaign & IEC Materials

Before any collection drive begins, ZGIH runs a targeted Information-Education-Communication (IEC) campaign in the community — educating residents on proper waste segregation, what materials will be collected, and the purpose of the program.

IEC Activities

Flyer and poster distribution in target barangays Loudspeaker announcements (barangay public address) Social media posts on ZGIH's pages School assembly presentations (for school programs) Purok-level orientations led by volunteer coordinators

Materials Produced

Branded tarpaulin and program banners (co-branded) Waste segregation guide (Filipino & Chavacano) Collection schedule cards distributed to households Partner company featured in all IEC materials
🙋

Step 3.3 Volunteer Recruitment & Orientation

ZGIH recruits and orients community volunteers — including youth groups, women's organizations, fisherfolk associations, and student organizations — who will assist in collection drives, sorting, and data recording.

Minimum of 20 trained volunteers per collection event Volunteer orientation covers safety protocols, waste identification, and data recording Volunteer certificates issued for participation (valued by youth and students) Corporate partner employees may join as volunteer participants (team-building option)
📋

Phase 3 Deliverables

  • Signed community endorsement/resolution from each target barangay
  • IEC materials produced and distributed (copies provided to partner)
  • Volunteer roster with signed participation agreements
  • Pre-program baseline waste audit report
  • Branded tarpaulins and event materials installed in target areas
PHASE 4

Month 1 onward (recurring)

Waste Collection Operations

This is the core of the program — organized, documented, and repeated collection drives that systematically divert plastic and solid waste from landfills, waterways, and coastal areas.

🗑️

Step 4.1 Collection Drive Execution

Each collection drive is a structured, half-day to full-day event. ZGIH coordinates logistics (vehicles, equipment, volunteer deployment), runs the event, and manages all waste documentation on-site.

Coastal clean-ups: Teams cover assigned shoreline sections, collecting and bagging debris, with marine debris classified separately Barangay collection: Household-to-household rounds along scheduled routes; residents pre-sort waste using ZGIH guides School programs: Student-led collection within school grounds, with eco-education component built in Event collection: ZGIH sets up zero-waste booths at partner-company events to capture waste on-site
⚖️

Step 4.2 On-Site Sorting & Weighing

All collected waste is sorted by material type and weighed on-site using calibrated weighing scales. This data is recorded in ZGIH's waste tracking system — the foundation of all impact documentation.

Material Category Resin / Code Disposition
PET Bottles#1♻️ Recycled
HDPE (containers)#2♻️ Recycled / 🛠️ Upcycled
Sachets / Multi-layer#7🛠️ Upcycled
Paper / Cardboard—♻️ Recycled / 🛠️ Upcycled
Aluminum / Metal—♻️ Sold to junk shop
Glass—♻️ Sold to recycler
Residual / Mixed—🗑️ Proper disposal
📸

Step 4.3 Real-Time Documentation

ZGIH's field documentation officer records all collection activities in real time — photographs, waste logs, volunteer counts, and GPS-tagged event locations. This raw data feeds directly into the partner's impact report.

Before-and-after photographs of each collection site (geotagged) Video documentation of collection events for partner communications use Volunteer headcount and participation logs Waste weight tallies by category entered into ZGIH's digital tracking system on the same day Waste certificates issued within 5 business days of each event
⚠️ For EPR programs: All weighing is done using DENR-compliant procedures. ZGIH maintains an official waste ledger that can be submitted directly to regulators as part of your annual EPR compliance report.
📋

Phase 4 Deliverables (per collection event)

  • Official Waste Diversion Certificate (signed by ZGIH Executive Director)
  • Event waste log: total weight by material category
  • Photo and video documentation package (high-resolution)
  • Volunteer and beneficiary count records
  • GPS-tagged event location data
PHASE 5

Ongoing — within 1 week per event

Waste Processing & Diversion

Collected waste doesn't just disappear — ZGIH tracks exactly where every kilogram goes. This transparent chain of custody is what makes our documentation credible for EPR compliance and ESG reporting.

🏭

Step 5.1 Waste Routing to Accredited Partners

Sorted waste is transported to ZGIH's network of accredited recyclers, junk shops, and upcycling workshops within Zamboanga City. Each disposal pathway is documented with receipts and weight tickets.

♻️ Recyclable Waste

PET bottles → accredited plastic recyclers Aluminum cans → scrap metal traders Paper/cardboard → paper recycling mills Glass → glass recyclers

🛠️ Upcyclable Waste

Sachets & multi-layer plastics → ZGIH upcycling workshop HDPE → woven bag production Paper → recycled stationery production Coconut shells & natural materials → décor items
🔗

Step 5.2 Chain of Custody Documentation

Every kilogram of waste is tracked from collection point to final disposal destination. ZGIH maintains a full chain of custody record — essential for EPR compliance and credible ESG reporting.

📄Collection weight ticket (signed on-site)
🏭Recycler/junk shop receipt & weight record
🚛Transport manifest (vehicle, driver, date)
📍Drop-off location records with address
📸Photo of waste handover at processing site
📊Running total in ZGIH's master waste ledger
📋 For EPR partners: This chain of custody documentation is formatted to meet DENR's requirements for EPR compliance reporting under RA 11898. Partners receive the full documentation package for their annual regulatory submission.
📋

Phase 5 Deliverables

  • Full chain of custody documentation for each batch of waste processed
  • Recycler receipts and weight confirmations
  • Updated running waste diversion total for partner dashboard
  • EPR-formatted waste records (for compliance programs)
PHASE 6

Ongoing — throughout program

Livelihood & Community Impact

This is what separates ZGIH from a simple waste collection service. The waste collected doesn't just disappear — it becomes income for families, education for students, and restored coastlines for fisherfolk. This phase tracks and delivers the human side of the impact.

👩‍🎨

Step 6.1 Upcycling Production & Artisan Employment

For programs that include livelihood components, upcyclable waste goes to ZGIH's community workshop where trained artisans — primarily women and youth — transform it into saleable eco-products. The partner's sponsorship funds artisan wages.

Each sponsored artisan receives a fixed monthly stipend funded by the program Products made include bags, décor items, paper products, and fashion accessories Artisan profiles and income data documented for partner's social impact report Partner may co-brand products or receive a batch of products as program deliverable Products sold in ZGIH's Green Store, with revenue cycling back to the community
🏫

Step 6.2 Eco-Education Delivery (if included)

For programs with an education component, ZGIH's Education Coordinator delivers structured eco-education sessions in partner schools — using ZGIH's DepEd-aligned curriculum on solid waste, plastics, and environmental action.

Sessions delivered by certified ZGIH educators in Grades 7–12 Content covers solid waste basics, plastics science, the 3Rs, and local environmental issues Student participation tracked (attendance sheets, pre/post quizzes) Partner company featured in classroom materials and certificates of participation School gardens and segregation systems set up (for extended programs)
🌳

Step 6.3 Conservation Activities (if included)

For programs with a conservation component, ZGIH coordinates mangrove planting, coral monitoring, and ongoing coastal stewardship with fisherfolk organizations. All restoration activities are geo-tagged and documented with survival rate tracking.

Mangrove seedlings sourced from DENR-accredited nurseries in Zamboanga Planting events organized with local fisherfolk groups as community stewards GPS coordinates recorded for every planted grove — survival rates tracked quarterly Partner company recognized with a named mangrove grove or restoration site
📋

Phase 6 Deliverables

  • Artisan employment records and income disbursement documentation
  • Products produced from program waste (count, type, photos)
  • School session attendance sheets, quiz results, and certificates
  • Mangrove planting records with GPS coordinates and survival tracking
  • Community beneficiary count and profile summaries
PHASE 7

Quarterly + End of Program

Impact Reporting & Program Review

Transparency is non-negotiable at ZGIH. Partners receive thorough, ESG-ready impact reports at regular intervals — and a comprehensive final report at program close that they can use directly in sustainability disclosures, investor reports, and regulatory submissions.

📊

Step 7.1 Quarterly Progress Reports

Every three months, ZGIH sends the partner a structured progress report covering all program KPIs, events completed, waste diverted, community beneficiaries reached, and budget utilization. A review call with the Program Officer is included.

⚖️Total waste collected (kg) YTD
♻️Waste diverted by material type
👥Beneficiaries & volunteers reached
🎯KPI progress vs. targets
📸Photo documentation gallery
💰Budget utilization summary
🔍

Step 7.2 Mid-Program Review (Month 6)

At the halfway point, ZGIH and the partner conduct a formal program review. If targets are off-track, program activities are adjusted. If the program is exceeding expectations, the partner may opt to expand scope or increase targets.

Review meeting with ZGIH Program Officer and corporate sustainability lead Assessment of KPI progress — on track, ahead, or behind Program amendments agreed upon and documented via a simple addendum to the MOA Mid-program press release or social media feature offered to partner (optional)
🔄 Flexibility: ZGIH programs are designed to adapt. If a typhoon disrupts collection in Month 4, we reschedule rather than report a miss. The mid-program review is where we make those adjustments formally.
📑

Step 7.3 Final Impact Report & Certificate

At program completion, ZGIH produces a comprehensive Final Impact Report — a polished, professionally formatted document that covers the full program lifecycle. This report is ESG-disclosure ready and accepted for DENR EPR submissions.

The Final Impact Report includes:

📊Full KPI summary vs. targets achieved
⚖️Total waste diverted (certified, by type)
👩‍🎨Artisan employment & livelihood data
🎓Students and community members reached
🌊Conservation outcomes (if applicable)
💵Full financial accountability summary
📷Full photo and video documentation
🗺️Program geography & community maps
🏅

Step 7.4 Program Recognition & Renewal

ZGIH publicly recognizes all program partners on our website, social media, and annual report. Partners receive a formal Certificate of Partnership and are given the option to renew, expand, or transition to a longer-term program.

Official Certificate of Partnership signed by ZGIH Executive Director Partner featured in ZGIH's Annual Impact Report and website Partners Directory Social media recognition post on ZGIH's platforms at program close Program renewal discussion initiated 60 days before program end date Returning partners receive priority scheduling and expanded barangay access
📋

Phase 7 Final Deliverables

  • Comprehensive Final Impact Report (PDF — ESG & EPR ready)
  • Official Certificate of Partnership (signed, suitable for framing)
  • Full waste diversion certificates (DENR-formatted for EPR programs)
  • Complete photo/video archive of all program activities
  • Chain of custody documentation for all waste processed
  • Program close social media recognition feature
