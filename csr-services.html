How It Works

The ZGIH process —
step by step

From first inquiry to final report, here is exactly how ZGIH designs and delivers every CSR program. No surprises, no vague deliverables.

💬

Phase 1 · Weeks 1–2

Discovery & Partnership Agreement

We start with a free 30-minute discovery call to understand your company's sustainability goals, preferred program type, budget range, and timeline. Once aligned, ZGIH prepares a Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) and assigns a dedicated Program Officer to your account.

Initial inquiry via website, Facebook, or email Discovery call with ZGIH Program Officer (within 2 business days) Custom program proposal sent within 5 business days MOA drafted, reviewed, and co-signed by both parties
📋

You receive: Signed MOA · Program overview document · Assigned Program Officer · Welcome kit with co-branding guidelines

⏱ Timeline: 1–2 weeks from first contact
🗺️

Phase 2 · Weeks 2–4

Program Design & Planning

ZGIH maps the target communities, sets clear KPIs aligned to your ESG framework, and builds a month-by-month activity calendar. We define exactly who is responsible for what — on both sides.

Mapping of target barangays, schools, or coastal areas Baseline waste audit to establish pre-program benchmarks KPI framework agreed (environmental, social, and reporting KPIs) 12-month activity calendar finalized and shared
📋

You receive: Program Operational Plan · Activity Calendar · KPI Framework · Target community map · Responsibility matrix

⏱ Timeline: 2–4 weeks after MOA signing
📢

Phase 3 · Weeks 3–5

Community Mobilization

ZGIH activates its community networks — engaging Barangay Captains, fisherfolk groups, school principals, and volunteer organizations. We run an IEC (Information-Education-Communication) campaign so residents understand the program before it begins.

Formal consultation with Barangay Captains and community leaders Barangay endorsement letters and resolutions secured IEC campaign: flyers, announcements, co-branded materials distributed Community volunteers recruited and oriented
📋

You receive: Community endorsement letters · IEC material copies · Volunteer roster · Co-branded tarpaulins installed in target areas

⏱ Timeline: Overlaps with Phase 2; completed before first event
🚀

Phase 4 · Month 1 onward (recurring)

Program Implementation

Collection drives, livelihood sessions, school workshops, or coastal restoration events are executed on schedule. Every event is documented in real time — weights, photos, volunteer counts, and GPS data — by ZGIH's field documentation officer.

Events executed per the activity calendar (monthly or quarterly) All waste sorted, weighed, and recorded on-site per material type Before-and-after photography and video captured at every event Waste transported to accredited recyclers with chain-of-custody records Waste Diversion Certificate issued within 5 business days per event
📋

Per event: Waste Diversion Certificate · Event waste log by material · Photo/video package · Volunteer count records · GPS-tagged location data

⏱ Timeline: Ongoing throughout the program period
📈

Phase 5 · Quarterly + End of Program

Impact Reporting & Program Close

Every three months, partners receive a structured progress report. At program close, ZGIH produces a comprehensive Final Impact Report — ESG-ready, with all certificates, photos, and data in one polished document. The partner is publicly recognized on ZGIH's platforms.

Quarterly reports with KPI progress, photos, and budget summary Mid-program review call at the 6-month mark (for annual programs) Final Impact Report delivered within 30 days of program close Certificate of Partnership signed by ZGIH Executive Director Partner featured on ZGIH website, social media, and annual report
📋

Final deliverables: Comprehensive Impact Report (ESG-ready) · Certificate of Partnership · Full waste certificates · Photo archive · Social media recognition

⏱ Final report: within 30 days of program end
